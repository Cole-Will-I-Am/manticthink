Logos — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 22, 2026

Logos is an end-to-end encrypted (E2EE) messenger. It is designed so that no one but you and the people you message — not even us — can read your messages. This policy explains what data exists, where it lives, and the few cases where data leaves your device.

Beta notice. Logos is experimental software in active development and has not yet undergone an independent security audit. Please don’t rely on it for high-stakes secrets until it has been audited.

The short version

  • No account, no personal data. Logos needs no email, phone number, or real name. Your identity is a cryptographic key generated on your device; your username is just a label you choose. There is no password and no profile.
  • Messages are end-to-end encrypted. Our relay server only ever sees encrypted ciphertext it cannot decrypt — never your message text, photos, or files.
  • Your data stays on your device. Chat history, contacts, nicknames, and photos are stored only on your phone, encrypted at rest, and excluded from device backups. They are never uploaded to us.
  • No analytics, ads, trackers, or third-party data-collection SDKs.
  • We do not sell or share your data, and we are technically unable to read your messages.

What the relay server can and cannot see

To deliver a message between two people, Logos uses a minimal relay server. The relay:

  • Cannot read message content, attachments, or AI content — everything is encrypted on your device before it is sent (PQXDH key agreement + Double Ratchet), and the sender is concealed from the relay using sealed-sender delivery.
  • Temporarily holds encrypted, opaque message envelopes only until the recipient’s device fetches them, after which they are deleted.
  • Stores the mapping from your chosen username to your public key, and a pool of one-time public “prekeys,” so others can start an encrypted conversation with you.
  • Necessarily processes limited routing metadata (such as the destination mailbox and timing) to deliver messages, as any messaging service must.

Data stored on your device

  • Your identity key and 24-word recovery phrase (the recovery phrase is shown only when you choose to back it up).
  • Your conversations and message history, encrypted at rest with a device-only key.
  • Contacts, nicknames, and any contact photos you add — all local and never shared.

If you delete a chat, its messages and any attached files are removed from your device. If you lose your device without your recovery phrase, that data cannot be recovered.

Optional AI features (off by default)

Logos includes optional AI features (such as “Catch me up” summaries and an in-app assistant). These run only if you turn them on, and you choose the provider:

  • On-device AI — runs entirely on your phone. Nothing leaves the device.
  • Bring-your-own-key (cloud) AI — if you add your own API key for a provider (e.g. Anthropic, OpenAI, or your own Ollama server), the specific content you ask the AI to process is sent directly from your device to that provider using your key. That content leaves end-to-end encryption and is handled under that provider’s privacy policy. It happens only per request, with an on-screen notice, and is never routed through the Logos relay. Your API keys are stored only on your device (in the system Keychain) and are never sent to us.

Third parties

  • Network transport is carried over Cloudflare; it conveys only encrypted traffic.
  • AI providers receive content only if you opt in to cloud AI with your own key, as described above.
  • Apple distributes the app (App Store / TestFlight) and provides standard platform services. Any data Apple collects is governed by Apple’s policies.

Children

Logos is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from them.
